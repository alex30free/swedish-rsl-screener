Strategy Overview

What is RSL?

The Levy Relative Strength (RSL) measures a stock's current price relative to its own 130-day moving average. A value above 1.0 means the stock trades above its 6-month average — a sign of sustained momentum.

How to use it

Buy the top-ranked stocks (highest RSL). Review and rebalance weekly. Sell any holding that drops out of the top tier or falls below its 130-day average (RSL < 1.0). Always combine with position sizing and risk management.

Why it works

Academic research confirms price momentum persists across 3–12 months. Stocks that have recently outperformed tend to continue outperforming — the RSL systematically identifies these leaders.

Updated

Every Friday evening after Nasdaq Stockholm closes (~18:30 CET). Data sourced automatically from Yahoo Finance via GitHub Actions.

RSL = Current Price ÷ 130-day Simple Moving Average

Piotroski F-Score — Quality Filter

J.D. Piotroski — "Value Investing: The Use of Historical Financial Statement Information to Separate Winners from Losers", Journal of Accounting Research (2000)

The F-Score assigns one point for each of 9 financial criteria across three dimensions. Combined with RSL momentum ranking, a high F-Score confirms the fundamental health behind the momentum signal — filtering out stocks that may be rising on speculative flows rather than genuine financial improvement.
